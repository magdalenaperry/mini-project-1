mexican conchas

Mexican Conchas are one of the trademark Mexican breads. They are very similar in consistency to a brioche and are topped with a shortbread cookie-like layer. They are called conchas, because of the 'shell' like pattern stamped on the top of each bread.


Ingredients:
  • 500g bread flour
  • 125g sugar
  • 1 packet active dry yeast
  • 3g salt
  • 100g non-salted butter
  • 100 ml eggs
  • 5 ml vanilla
  • 120 ml warm milk

Mix flour, sugar, and salt in a large mixing bowl. In a measuring cup, mix all wet ingredients and add yeast. Let yeast ferment for approximately 5 minutes. Add fermented yeast mixture into bread bowl and mix on medium speed for approximately 8-10 minutes. Dough is ready when it is smooth and elastic. Let dough rise until doubled. In another bowl, mix shortening, flour, and cocoa powder until it is shortbread consistency. Divide into 12 portions. After dough has risen, punch through the dough and create 12 rounded dough balls. Take the shortbread portions and roll into thin discs to place and press down over your dough balls. Allow dough balls to proof for 50 minutes. Using a knife cut into the shortbread topping to create patterns. Bake at 350 degrees for 20 minutes.
